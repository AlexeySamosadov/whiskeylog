🥃 WhiskeyLog · whisky tasting & collection

A whisky tasting journal that remembers every dram for you

Taste enough whisky and the drams start to blur together — was the Springbank the one with the brine and pencil shavings, or was that the other Campbeltown? WhiskeyLog is a tasting journal built for that problem. After each pour you jot the nose, palate and finish and give it a score out of 100 — the scale serious whisky communities actually use — and the app keeps a tidy, dated history under each bottle.

Because every note is timestamped and attached to the bottle, you build something genuinely useful over time: a personal reference of what you've tried, what you'd buy again, and how your palate is changing. Re-reading your own words from a year ago — "long, warming finish, lingering peat and dark chocolate" — is half the fun, and it stops you re-buying a bottle you didn't actually like. No account, nothing uploaded; the journal lives in your browser and works offline, so you can log a dram at a tasting with no signal.
